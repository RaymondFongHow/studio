可乐 · 盛夏 Carbonate-Summer, May 2021

In the timely spring rain, all creatures are nourished, awakening from their slumber. People marvel at the gentle drizzle and praise the might of the deluge. Standing in rain, I found myself pondering: how would rain appear through my lens? What other forms does it take? In Singapore, where four seasons are absent, how does rain possess its own unique charm? How would the falling raindrops stir the depths of people's minds? These musings inspired me to curate a collection of photographs taken over the past two years, capturing the essence of rain in all its forms - be it the falling rain, the lively splashes and ripples of raindrops, the graceful dance of snow, or the ephemeral beauty of rainbows that emerge after storms. With that, I'm thrilled to present to you the photo album Precipitation.
